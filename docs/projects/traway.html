Traway Operations Analytics

Cohort analysis, pricing segmentation, and retention modeling for a student-led mobility startup — turning 3,200 customers into actionable growth levers.

Timeline Dec 2022 – Jul 2024
Role Co-founder & CEO
Stack Python · SQL · Regression · EDA
Outcome 60%+ retention · 3× AOV lift
01 / Overview

What is Traway?

Traway was a student-led mobility and events startup based in Srinagar, India. As Co-founder and CEO, I built and scaled the venture alongside TheLoudKitchens, leading a cross-functional team of 10+ members and executing 175+ events over 18 months.

The analytics work focused on understanding customer behaviour at the cohort level — who stayed, who churned, and what pricing interventions could move the needle on retention and order value.

02 / Problem

The retention problem

With 3,000+ customers acquired across two ventures, we had significant volume but no systematic view of which cohorts returned, at what frequency, and why certain pricing tiers drove higher lifetime value.

Without data-driven segmentation, every marketing and pricing decision was intuition-based — and the gap between our best and worst cohorts was wide.

03 / Approach

Cohort analysis + pricing regression

  • Built cohort retention tables segmented by acquisition channel and event type using Python and SQL.
  • Ran regression analysis to quantify the relationship between pricing tiers, event size, and customer LTV.
  • Applied RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation across 3,000+ customer records.
  • Identified high-value segments and built a pricing ladder that reduced churn in the top cohort by targeting repeat-purchase incentives.
  • Used EDA to surface seasonal patterns and inform event scheduling decisions.
04 / Results

Measurable business impact

60%+ Customer retention rate
3× Increase in average order value (TheLoudKitchens)
175+ Events executed over 18 months

The segmentation work directly informed pricing decisions that tripled AOV for TheLoudKitchens and established a repeatable retention playbook for Traway.

05 / Learnings

What I would do differently

At the time, data collection was opportunistic rather than designed. With more instrumentation from day one — event-level tracking, referral source tagging — the cohort analysis would have been far more precise. This shaped how I think about analytics infrastructure in every subsequent project.
